Noteable Landmarks and Places to Check Out!

Singapore was recently voted the most instagramable city in the world (!!!) and much of that honour can be attributed to the endless number of famous landmarks that it has to offer. Some of its landmarks like the Merlion and the Marina Bay Sands hotel are amongst the most recognisable monuments in the world. As one of Asia's cultural hubs, Singapore is a melting pot of different cultures and tradtions which is well reflected in its landmarks. Along with this there are many hidden gems like Haji lane and the Asian Civilizations Museum that this guide will briefly touch on to make sure you are getting the full Singaporean experience on your trip. Many of these landmarks can be visited on the same day and for free so try and check out as many as you can!

The arts & science museum is the largest and most notable museum in the country. A location that all tourists coming to Singapore should visit to gain a better appreciation for its scientific and artistic contributions to the world.

Another one of Singapore's museums; the Asian civilizations museum gives visitors an in-depth view into the country's cultural and societal history. Many of the exhibits contain rare artifacts that blow away visitors.

Haji lane is a quaint alleyway that is full of stores that sell local goods and nic nacs. Come for the affordable shopping or just to meet new people and learn about their lives over a cool lassi drink.

Gardens by the Bay is one of the most scenic spots in the entire country. It is a nature park spannning 101 hectares and was recently inscribed as the world's only tropical botanic garden on the UNESCO World Heritage Site list. Come for a morning stroll and stay for the evening lightshow.

Ion orchard is Singapore's premier shopping mall located right in the heart of the city. It boasts an impressive list of designer brands and gives tourists the opportunity to spend their money on an item to remember the trip by, in a mall that they will likely never forget.

Marina Bay Sands is at the center of the Singapore skyline and is so much more than just a hotel. In its basement is an expansive casino, the ground floor is a large shopping mall, and the top floor is an infinity pool inside the famous boat design. Even if you decide not to stay at the hotel, the view of the building can be enjoyed from almost anywhere in the city.

The Masjid Sultan is a national monument and an active mosque where thousands of practicing Muslims go to pray on a daily basis. The mosque's natural beauty also attracts many vistors and gives them the opportunity to learn about and take part in the daily prayers.

The Merlion is arguably the most iconic Singaporean landmark. The lionhead is representative of Singapore's origional name (Singapura) which means lion city in Sanskrit. The fish-like body symbolises Singapore's origions as a fishing city. Truly a sight to see.

The Pelangi pride center is home to Singapore's largest LGBTQ community. They host a number of different events to advocate for LGBTQ rights and to raise awarness for the issues that the community faces in Singapore. Make sure to go down to their center to meet the members in a safe and welcoming environment.

The rain vortex at Jewel is a new additional to the city and is an extension to the world famous Changi airport. This man-made waterfall is a great place to take pictures at the start and end of your trip!

The Singapore flyer is one of the largest giant observation wheels in the world and is the perfect way to get a view of the entire city. While the wheel moves a little too slowly for many people's taste, there is so much you can do while you are on the attraction such as get a meal with friends as shown by the photo.

The Thian Hock Kheng temple is one of the most important Chinese historical monuments in the country and remains the oldest and most important temple of the Hokkien people. While it appears to be relatively small from the inside, I would encourage you to explore it in person and see all the different rooms and statues that are present inside the temple.
