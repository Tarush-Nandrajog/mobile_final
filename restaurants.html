The Best Places in Singapore to Grab a Bite!

One of the most exciting parts of Singapore is the wide array of local and international cuisine that is served at almost 8000 different food & beverage establishments. Some local dishes that are a must try for tourists are Chilli crab, Laksa, and my personal favourite, Haianese chicken rice. Popular vegetarian options also include nasi lemak (fragrant rice and side dishes served in a pandan leaf) and mee goreng (fried noodles). This page will detail some of the best places in the country to try out these local specialities along with other dishes from cultures all around the world.

The corner house is a michelin star restaurant fresh restaurant located in the magnificant botanical gardens which as of 2021 is officially a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The beautiful view along with the delicious food makes it a must-visit restaurant.

The genesis vegan restaurant located in the heart of the city offers exclusively vegan dishes for anyone with more particular dietary restrictions looking to grab a quick bite.

This is just one of the many hawker centers located all around Singapore which is our version of the sterotypical food court. The meals here are far more affordable than typical restaurants and are a great way to experience the true local flavours and lifestyle.

Keng Eng Kee Seafood is a very small and up-and-coming establishment, but one I visit whenever I am craving a tasty seafood dish.

Another michelin star restaurant for those looking to splurge on a night out and experience the highest-class local food that Singapore has to offer. A great place to go to round off your Singaporean expedition.

Muchachos is a mexican restaurant and one of my personal favourites. The laid-back atmosphere combined with the delightful gourmet food is guaranteed to leave you full and satisfied.

PS cafe has rapidly gained notoriety as one of the best brunch and bar places in Singapore. It is also as gay-friendly as a restaurant can be which has made it a very popular location for people in the LGBTQ community to hangout after work.

Rang mahal is a fine-dining restaurant in the East Coast of Singapore which offers a variety of popular Indian dishes. The friendly nature of the staff and ownership allows you to form a real bond which will keep you coming back for more.

Soulfood is one of the few African-American inspired restaurants in Singapore and is a must-visit location. All of my many experiences at the restaurant have been overwhelmingly positive and it gives tourists and locals an insight into one of Singapore's most niche but important communities.

Koma is the epitome of fine dining and luxuary. An upscale Japanese restaurant & sushi bar specializing in serving gourmet food, cocktails, & sake, it is a must try for those with the budget and taste for Asia's finest. The dramatic, Japanese-inspired decor rounds off what will be an extraordinary dining experience.
